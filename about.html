What We Do

UK Debt Solutions We Can Help With

We help UK residents explore and access regulated debt solutions. We help you understand what each option means for your life — your credit file, your assets, your employment — before you make any decisions. No pressure, no hard sell, and no obligation.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

A formal, legally binding debt solution allowing you to repay what you can afford over a fixed period — typically 5 to 6 years — with any remaining eligible debt written off at the end.

Learn more

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

An informal arrangement where you make one affordable monthly payment, distributed to your creditors at a reduced rate. Available UK-wide with no minimum debt level and no legal process.

Learn more

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

A low-cost solution for people with low income, few assets, and debts under £30,000 — providing a 12-month moratorium on debt repayment, after which qualifying debts are written off.

Learn more

Bankruptcy

A formal legal process that can provide a fresh financial start when other solutions aren't suitable. Most debts are written off after typically 12 months. Available in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

Learn more

Protected Trust Deed

A formal debt solution for Scottish residents, similar in structure to an IVA. Supervised by a licensed trustee, typically running for 4 years, with remaining unsecured debt written off on completion.

Learn more

Sequestration

The Scottish equivalent of bankruptcy, providing a legal route to debt relief for eligible residents — including the Minimal Assets Process (MAP) for those with low income and few assets.

Learn more

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Us

Quick answers to the most common questions people ask before getting in touch.

Is Debt Free Path a charity?

No. We are a private debt advisory and referral service. However, our initial consultation is always free of charge. If you are looking for free charitable debt advice, we can also signpost you to organisations such as StepChange, Citizens Advice, and National Debtline.

Is Debt Free Path regulated by the FCA?

Debt Free Path operates as an introducer and referral service. All debt solutions we refer clients to are handled by FCA-authorised and regulated partners. We are committed to full compliance with UK financial regulations.

Will speaking to you affect my credit score?

No. Simply contacting us or receiving a debt assessment will not impact your credit rating. A credit search will only occur if and when you choose to proceed with a specific formal debt solution through one of our regulated partners.

Do you charge any fees?

There is no charge for your initial consultation or assessment. If you proceed with a debt solution through one of our regulated partners, fees may apply depending on the solution chosen — these will always be clearly explained before you agree to anything.

Do you cover Scotland?

Yes. We help clients across England, Wales, and Scotland. Please note that some debt solutions — such as Protected Trust Deed and Sequestration — are specific to Scotland and governed by Scottish law. Our advisers will guide you to the right option for your location.
